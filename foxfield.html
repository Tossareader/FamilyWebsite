Internet research reveals a number of references to a Hugh McMaster living at Foxfield, Clonalig in the 18th and early 19th century.  He is mentioned as a witness to an indenture of a lease for the Donaldson family of Castle Dillon in 1740. He is listed in the 1766 Relgious Census. Tomas O'Fiaich in an article in Seancas Ard  Mhacha,"Art MacCooey and His Times"  Vol. 6, No. 2 (1972), refers to him or his son, also Hugh (1741-1832), as having leased a portion of his land on at Clonalig in 1786 to Michael O'Callaghan, one of the O'Callaghans who owned the brewery at Culloville. “In 1786 Michael secured a lease of portion of Clonalig from his neighbour Hugh McMaster of Foxfield”

Hugh McMaster, the son, is buried in Creggan Churchard.      
His gravestone inscription reads:


                                            Sacred to the memory of Hugh McMaster esquire
                                             late of Foxfield in this parish who departed this life
                                             on the 3rd day of March 1832 aged 91 years,
                                             and also his daughter Eliza who died
                                             on the 4th day of August aged 49 years.


The notes accompanying this entry on the Creggan History Society’s website listing the grave inscriptions tell us that in 1799, he was appointed a Lieutenant of the Creggan Yeomanry and, on 15th October 1803, he was appointed a Lieutenant of the Armagh Cavalry.


We move on 30 years to 1864, the date of Griffiths Valuation which listed the inhabitants of all properties, their acreage and value. There is now a Mary Murphy living in Foxfield. See the relevant page in Griffiths for her name and map ref 67.  Foxfield at has the 2nd highest rateable value in Clonalig (£28). The 3rd highest is £13. Ivy Lodge at £42 is the highest - more about Ivy Lodge later.


So when did the Murphys come to live in Foxfield and how did it come about?  Remember they are still renting. Family lore has it that they came from Forkhill.  See the Murphy Foxfield  page for more information

Five years after Hugh McMasters’s death In the 1837 Townland Valuation for Clonalig, we have his daughter Catherine living at Foxfield. Only houses of value £5 or more a year were listed in this valuation and for Clonalig there were only two houses listed, Foxfield and Ivy Lodge. The two residents being Catherine McMaster (Foxfield) and Thomas O’Callaghan (Ivy Lodge),

However the Ordnance Survey Namebook of some years later  has surgeon McParlan iving there. The discrepancy of dates is probably accounted for by the distance in time between fact finding and writing of a document and its eventual publication.  



Presumably Catherine moved out of Foxfield mid 1830



Sacred to the memory of Catherine McMaster formerly of Foxfield in the County of Armagh who departed this life on the 27th day of September 1851 aged 75 years, and also of her sister Mary McMaster aged 55 years.

There is further reference to Catherine on a gravestone in St Nicholas Churchyard, Dundalk
