We know little of Henry Lennan’s daughter, Bridget. She died in 1854, 2 years before her niece, Catherine Lennon-Murphy (our great grandmother) was born.  Perhaps she was the eldest of the family.  It is through her great grand-daughter, Mary Teresa McIver that we have information about her descendants, namely her own branch of the family through her grandfather John. John McIver was a doctor in Ardee, married to Mary Teresa Dolan. Dr John McIver died in 1875 aged 45 when their only son, Joseph John was 6 years old. He, in his turn, died at 36 when his only daughter, the writer of the above family tree, was still a baby.  John McIver died the year his cousin, Catherine Lennon, married Bernard Murphy


The 1911 census show Mary Teresa Dolan-McIver aged 79 still living in Ardee.  On the census there is a Lucy McIver living next door to Mary Theresa, with a daughter, Mary Teresa age 7. This has to be her grand-daughter and the writer of the page given to us. Mary Teresa McIver married Charles Holmes from Parteen, Co Clare in 1930 and presumably went to live with him in the outskirts of Limerick.  She seems to be the last of that line unless she had children.  Mary Teresa must have gone researching her family tree or followed up on research she found among her father’s papers. He was a solicitor.  She must have been the person who had the the inscription added to the back of the McIver tombstone  in Creggan Churchyard. It can't have been her father, since he is mentioned on it.


It is possible that she added to an original tombstone erected by her father or grandfather which reads:







To the back of the tombstone, Mary Teresa has added








This tombstone is to be found in Section 2 in Creggan Churchyard. Copies the text of it is available to us thanks to the work of the Creggan History Society who have made copies of all legible tombstones.  Additional information has been added by the Society:




IHS

Of your charity pray for the repose of the soul of John McIver MD of Ardee who died on the 12th of August 1875 aged 45 years, and also of his son Joseph J  McIver (solicitor) who died 4th Octr 1905 aged 36 years and of his wife Mary Theresa McIver who died 19th Octr 1914 aged  79 years; RIP

Of your Charity pray for the souls of Terence McIver of Carnally who died on the 11th  of January 1874 and his wife Bridget who died on the 12th of May 1854; parents of John McIver MD of Ardee; also of their children Mary and Terence all whose remains are underneath interred - RIP

This headstone, inscribed on both sides, is a memorial to Dr John McIver, Ardee, and to other members of his family who  lived at Ardee and Carnally. In “Seanchas Ardmhacha”, in 1972 Cardinal Tomas O’Fiaich wrote: “the later descendants of this family have spelled the surname of both MacIvor and MacKeever. The medical tradition must go back a long way in the family as An Doctuir MacIomhair is mentioned Seamus MacCuarta’s poem on Peggy Markey’s ale house at Drumbilla in the early 18th century. The Doctor was evidently a well-know “Character” and some quatrains in an Art Bennet MS. in St  Patrick’s College, Armagh, are attributed to him.... “Bridget McIver was a sister of Fr Michael Lennon, PP of Upper Creggan from 1829 to 1871. Buried here also are the parents of Fr Nicholas McIvor, PP of Forkhill from 1790 to 1827.
