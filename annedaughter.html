The next mention we have of Anne is 30 years later on 5th February 1880 in the parish registry when her marriage to Henry Colgan from the townland of Aghayalloge is recorded.  He was 29, she was 30. Patrick, who dies two years later in 1882, doesn’t include her in the will he makes in 1881 presumably as she was now married.  See below the records of Anne’s  marriage. Her sister Rose was the bridesmaid.

Anne Connellan, our great grand-aunt was the first child born to Patrick and Mary and obviously called after her grand-mother Anne Connellan, Patrick’s mother. See below her entry in the parish register for 24th April 1851.         

Some time before 1893 Anne dies leaving him with two daughters, Rose Ellen and Mary Anne.


In 1893 Henry marries again. He marries Mary Haughey from Meigh when Rose Ellen is 8 and Mary Anne 6.

Owen Rice is again Henry’s best man.  

The Colgan family kept in touch with the Connellan and later to be Murphy family in Meigh. Both families are mentioned as relatives in newspaper accounts of funerals.

Henry dies in February 1909. See below the death certificate, will abstract and the newspaper mention of his death and will.

John Connellan, his brother-in-law is one of his executors, the other is his son-in-law Hugh O’Hare.

The 1911 census has Mary, Henry’s 2nd wife living alone in the family home with 5 of their 6 children, two more having been born since the previous census. Bridget, aged 5 in the 1901 census is not listed for the 1911 census. A further search gives us a Bridget Colgan aged 15 in the census for the Sacred Heart boarding school in Armagh, the same school as Mamie Connellan, her cousin by marriage was in. They are both on the list. Bridget could well be in the school class photo we have of Mamie since they are about the same age.

   Bridget Colgan married Patrick Murphy of Drumintee.  See newspaper account of her funeral in 1947 (for checking connections)
